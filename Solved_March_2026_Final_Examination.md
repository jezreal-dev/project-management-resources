### Section A: Objective Questions (40 Marks)
Answer ALL questions. Choose the correct option (A–D).

#### 1. A project is:
* **Correct Answer: B. A temporary endeavour that creates a unique product or service**
* *Justification:* By definition, a project is temporary (has a defined start and end date) and unique (its deliverables differ from operations).

#### 2. Which of the following is NOT a project management process group?
* **Correct Answer: C. Marketing**
* *Justification:* The five process groups are Initiation, Planning, Execution, Monitoring/Controlling, and Closing. Marketing is a business function, not a project process group.

#### 3. The document that officially authorizes a project is called:
* **Correct Answer: B. Project Charter**
* *Justification:* The Project Charter is the official document signed by the sponsor that authorizes the project and gives the PM authority to use organizational resources.

#### 4. Which knowledge area deals with defining project work?
* **Correct Answer: B. Scope Management**
* *Justification:* Scope Management processes ensure the project includes all the work required, and only the work required, to complete the project successfully.

#### 5. WBS means:
* **Correct Answer: A. Work Breakdown Structure**
* *Justification:* WBS stands for Work Breakdown Structure, which is the hierarchical decomposition of the project scope.

#### 6. Scope creep refers to:
* **Correct Answer: B. Uncontrolled expansion of project scope**
* *Justification:* Scope creep is the expansion of project scope without adjustments to time, cost, or resource allocations.

#### 7. Which tool is commonly used for scheduling project activities?
* **Correct Answer: B. Gantt Chart**
* *Justification:* A Gantt Chart is a bar chart used to visually schedule and track project activities over time.

#### 8. The Critical Path consists of activities with:
* **Correct Answer: B. Zero float**
* *Justification:* Activities on the critical path have zero flexibility (float = 0) because any delay immediately delays the project end date.

#### 9. Float is:
* **Correct Answer: B. Extra time available for an activity**
* *Justification:* Float represents the duration an activity can be delayed without impacting successor tasks or the project finish date.

#### 10. Which knowledge area focuses on budgeting?
* **Correct Answer: A. Cost Management**
* *Justification:* Cost Management involves estimating costs, determining the budget, and controlling expenditures.

#### 11. Planned Value (PV) is:
* **Correct Answer: B. Budgeted value of planned work**
* *Justification:* Planned Value is the authorized budget assigned to the work scheduled to be completed by a specific date.

#### 12. Earned Value (EV) represents:
* **Correct Answer: B. Value of completed work**
* *Justification:* Earned Value is the budgeted value of the work actually completed by a specific date.

#### 13. Actual Cost (AC) refers to:
* **Correct Answer: B. Money actually spent**
* *Justification:* Actual Cost is the realized cost incurred for the work performed on project activities.

#### 14. Cost Variance (CV) is calculated as:
* **Correct Answer: A. EV - AC**
* *Justification:* Cost Variance compares the value of the completed work against what was actually spent ($CV = EV - AC$).

#### 15. A positive Cost Variance indicates that the project is:
* **Correct Answer: B. Under budget**
* *Justification:* A positive Cost Variance ($CV > 0$) means the value of the completed work is higher than the money spent, representing a cost savings.

#### 16. CPI stands for:
* **Correct Answer: A. Cost Performance Index**
* *Justification:* CPI is the ratio of earned value to actual cost ($EV / AC$), measuring cost efficiency.

#### 17. Which document contains identified project risks?
* **Correct Answer: B. Risk Register**
* *Justification:* The Risk Register lists all identified risks, their analyses, owners, and planned response strategies.

#### 18. A stakeholder is:
* **Correct Answer: B. Anyone affected by the project**
* *Justification:* Anyone who can affect, be affected by, or perceive themselves to be affected by the project is a stakeholder.

#### 19. Which communication method involves meetings?
* **Correct Answer: C. Interactive Communication**
* *Justification:* Meetings involve a real-time, two-way exchange of information, which is the definition of interactive communication.

#### 20. The communication channels formula is:
* **Correct Answer: B. n(n-1)/2**
* *Justification:* The standard formula to calculate communication channels for $n$ stakeholders is $n(n-1)/2$.

#### 21. Quality Assurance focuses on:
* **Correct Answer: B. Preventing defects**
* *Justification:* Quality Assurance (Manage Quality) is process-oriented, focusing on preventing defect introduction during execution.

#### 22. Quality Control focuses on:
* **Correct Answer: A. Inspection of outputs**
* *Justification:* Quality Control is product-oriented, focusing on testing and inspecting the deliverables to identify defects.

#### 23. Risk is:
* **Correct Answer: B. An uncertain event that may affect objectives**
* *Justification:* A risk is defined as an uncertain future event that has a positive or negative impact on project objectives if it occurs.

#### 24. EMV means:
* **Correct Answer: A. Expected Monetary Value**
* *Justification:* EMV stands for Expected Monetary Value, a statistical calculation representing the value of uncertain outcomes.

#### 25. EMV is calculated by:

* *Justification:* EMV is calculated by multiplying the probability of a risk occurrence by its financial impact.

#### 26. Procurement involves:
* **Correct Answer: B. Purchasing goods and services**
* *Justification:* Procurement is the process of acquiring products, services, or results from outside the project team.

#### 27. Which contract transfers most cost risk to the seller?
* **Correct Answer: B. Fixed Price**

#### 28. Scrum is:
* **Correct Answer: B. An Agile framework**

#### 29. A Sprint is:

#### 30. The Scrum Master is responsible for:

#### 33. Which organizational structure gives the project manager the highest authority?

### Section B: Theory Questions
Answer Question One (1) Compulsorily and Any Other Two (2).

#### 1. (Compulsory)
* **(a) Define Project Management. (3 Marks)**
  * *Solution:* Project Management is the application of knowledge, skills, tools, and techniques to project activities to meet project requirements and objectives. It ensures that project work is planned, organized, executed, monitored, and completed successfully within established constraints.
* **(b) Explain the five phases of the Project Life Cycle. (7 Marks)**
  * *Solution:* 
    1. *Initiation:* The project is formally authorized and defined. The Project Charter is signed, and key stakeholders are identified.
    2. *Planning:* The project blueprint is developed. Scope, cost, schedule, risk, and quality are planned and integrated into the Project Management Plan.
    3. *Execution:* The team carries out the work packages defined in the plan to build deliverables.
    4. *Monitoring and Controlling:* The PM tracks performance against baselines, manages changes, and takes corrective actions. (Continuous).
    5. *Closing:* Handovers are completed, contracts closed, resources released, and lessons learned registers are finalized.

#### 2. Explain the concept of Project Scope and discuss the importance of the Work Breakdown Structure (WBS).
* **Solution:**
  * *Project Scope* is the work required, and only the work required, to complete the project successfully. It outlines boundaries by defining what is in-scope and what is out-of-scope.
  * *WBS Importance:*
    * Establishes the Scope Baseline (along with the Scope Statement and WBS Dictionary).
    * Decomposes complex deliverables into manageable Work Packages, allowing accurate cost, resource, and duration estimations.
    * Prevents scope creep by setting clear boundaries on what work is approved.
    * Provides a structured framework for performance tracking (e.g., EVM).

#### 3. Discuss Stakeholder Management. Who is a stakeholder, and why is stakeholder engagement important to project success?
* **Solution:**
  * A *stakeholder* is any individual, group, or organization that can affect, be affected by, or perceive itself to be affected by the decisions, activities, or outcomes of a project.
  * *Importance of Stakeholder Engagement:*
    * Ensures all requirements are gathered correctly, preventing late scope changes.
    * Builds trust and support, converting resistant stakeholders into neutral or supportive allies.
    * Prevents project failure due to poor communication or unmet expectations.
    * Facilitates smooth sign-off and formal acceptance during deliverables handover.

#### 4. Define Earned Value Management (EVM). Explain the meaning of PV, EV, AC, CV, and SV.
* **Solution:**
  * *EVM* is a project performance measurement technique that integrates scope, schedule, and cost metrics to assess progress and forecast outcomes.
  * *Terms:*
    * **PV (Planned Value):** Budgeted cost of scheduled work.
    * **EV (Earned Value):** Budgeted value of work actually completed.
    * **AC (Actual Cost):** Real money spent on completed work.
    * **CV (Cost Variance):** Budget performance ($EV - AC$). Negative means over budget.
    * **SV (Schedule Variance):** Schedule performance ($EV - PV$). Negative means behind schedule.

#### 5. Discuss Project Risk Management and explain four common risk response strategies.
* **Solution:**
  * *Risk Management* is the proactive process of identifying, analyzing, planning, and implementing responses to project uncertainties to increase positive opportunities and decrease negative threats.
  * *Four Risk Response Strategies (Threats):*
    1. **Avoid:** Eliminate the risk completely (e.g., change the project plan or cancel a high-risk scope item).
    2. **Mitigate:** Reduce the probability or impact of the risk (e.g., perform extra testing or hire an expert).
    3. **Transfer:** Shift ownership of the impact to a third party (e.g., purchase insurance or outsource to a vendor).
    4. **Accept:** Take no active measures. Active acceptance involves setting up a Contingency Reserve. Passive acceptance involves addressing the issue with workarounds if it occurs.

#### 6. Explain Agile Project Management. Discuss the roles of the Product Owner, Scrum Master, and Development Team.
* **Solution:**
  * *Agile* is an iterative, incremental approach to project management that focuses on flexibility, short development cycles (sprints), customer feedback, and team self-organization.
  * *Scrum Roles:*
    * **Product Owner (PO):** Represents the business and customer. Manages the Product Backlog, prioritizing requirements to maximize product value.
    * **Scrum Master:** Servant leader who facilitates meetings, guides the team on Agile practices, and removes blockers/impediments.
    * **Development Team:** A self-organizing, cross-functional group that does the actual work to build the sprint deliverable.

#### Question 1: Project Planning Scenario
*You have just been appointed as the Project Manager for the construction of a new ICT Training Centre in your community. Explain how you would carry out the following activities before the project begins:*

* **(a) Develop the Project Charter:**
  * *Action:* I will collaborate with the Project Sponsor to draft the charter. I will define the project purpose (to improve local digital literacy), document high-level requirements, outline major deliverables (classrooms, computers, network cabling), assign my role and authority, define high-level risks, and establish the summary budget and milestone dates. The document will be signed by the Project Sponsor to formally authorize the project.
* **(b) Identify the project stakeholders:**
  * *Action:* I will conduct a stakeholder analysis. I will identify all key players (local community leaders, students, construction vendors, government inspectors, and the project sponsor). I will record their details, interest, and power in the Stakeholder Register. I will then categorize them on the Power/Interest Grid (e.g., community leaders as "Keep Informed," government inspectors as "Keep Satisfied," and the sponsor as "Manage Closely").
* **(c) Prepare the Work Breakdown Structure (WBS):**
  * *Action:* I will decompose the total scope statement into deliverables. I will structure the WBS hierarchically (e.g., 1.0 Foundation, 2.0 Structural Building, 3.0 ICT Infrastructure, 4.0 Handover). I will break these down to the lowest level (Work Packages, such as 3.1 Network Cabling, 3.2 PC Installation). I will also draft a WBS Dictionary containing detailed criteria and descriptions for each package.
* **(d) Identify possible project risks:**
  * *Action:* I will host a risk identification session with the team and local representatives. We will brainstorm potential risks (e.g., material delivery delays, community protests, power supply issues). I will log these in the Risk Register, assess their probability and impact (Qualitative Analysis), and outline high-level responses (e.g., securing a backup power generator to mitigate power failure risks).
* **(e) Develop a communication plan:**
  * *Action:* I will document stakeholder information needs. I will define what reports are sent, to whom, when, and how. I will implement:
    * *Interactive:* Weekly meetings with construction supervisors.
    * *Push:* Monthly email status reports to the sponsor and community leaders.
    * *Pull:* Maintaining a shared online dashboard where inspectors can view compliance certifications.

#### Question 2: Project Failure Scenario
*A company started a six-month project to develop a mobile application. After three months, the project is behind schedule, the budget has exceeded expectations, stakeholders are dissatisfied, and team members are experiencing conflicts. Explain the steps you would take as the Project Manager:*

* **(a) Identify the major problems affecting the project:**
  * *Action:* I will conduct a variance analysis using Earned Value Management (EVM) to calculate the Cost Variance (CV) and Schedule Variance (SV). This will quantify the exact budget overrun and timeline delay. I will then review the issue log, project requirements, and scope baseline to check for scope creep, and conduct individual interviews with team members and stakeholders to isolate the root causes of the distress.
* **(b) Resolve the team conflict:**
  * *Action:* I will bring the conflicting team members together in a private environment. I will facilitate **Collaborating (Problem Solving)** by asking them to focus on objective data, identify the root cause of their disagreement, and work together to find a win-win consensus that aligns with the project goals. I will discourage any forcing (win-lose) or avoiding behaviors.
* **(c) Manage stakeholder expectations:**
  * *Action:* I will be honest and transparent, adhering to the PMI Code of Ethics. I will present the EVM data and the root-cause analysis to the stakeholders. I will communicate the recovery options, negotiate adjustments to the project constraints (e.g., seeking approval from the CCB to extend the deadline or adjust the budget), and update the Stakeholder Engagement Plan to ensure regular, structured updates.
* **(d) Bring the project back on track:**
  * *Action:* I will implement schedule compression techniques:
    * *Crashing:* Adding resources to critical path activities (using the Contingency Reserve to fund extra developer hours).
    * *Fast-Tracking:* Performing critical path tasks in parallel that were originally planned in sequence (e.g., coding database elements while UI mockups are finalized), accepting the risk of potential rework.
  * I will strictly control the scope baseline to prevent any further scope creep.
* **(e) Prevent similar problems in future projects:**
  * *Action:* I will conduct a comprehensive lessons learned session at the end of the project and document all findings in the Lessons Learned Register (updating the company's Organizational Process Assets). I will advocate for thorough upfront planning, clearer WBS dictionaries, early stakeholder identification, and proactive risk management in future project initiations.

#### Question 3: Agile Project Scenario
*A software company has decided to use the Agile (Scrum) framework to develop an online banking application. As the Scrum Master, explain:*

* **(a) The roles of the Product Owner, Scrum Master, and Development Team:**
  * *PO:* Manages and prioritizes the Product Backlog, acts as the voice of the customer, and defines the acceptance criteria for each user story.
  * *Scrum Master:* Servant leader who facilitates meetings, ensures Agile processes are followed, and protects the team from external distractions while removing blockers.
  * *Development Team:* Cross-functional, self-organizing group of developers and testers who do the actual coding and verification to deliver a working product increment at the end of each sprint.
* **(b) How a Sprint is planned and executed:**
  * *Planning:* The team meets for Sprint Planning. The PO presents the highest-priority backlog items. The team estimates their capacity and selects the items they can commit to completing during the sprint, moving them to the Sprint Backlog and defining the Sprint Goal.
  * *Execution:* The team works to build the items. The Scrum Master protects the team and removes blockers. At the end of the sprint, the team holds a Sprint Review (demonstrating the working software to the PO and stakeholders for feedback) and a Sprint Retrospective (reviewing processes to improve the next sprint).
* **(c) The importance of Daily Stand-up Meetings:**
  * *Action:* These meetings are time-boxed to 15 minutes daily. They are crucial for peer-to-peer alignment. Each team member answers three questions:
    1. What did I do yesterday?
    2. What will I do today?
    3. What blockers are in my way?
  * This ensures transparency, exposes bottlenecks early, and allows the Scrum Master to immediately clear blockers to protect the schedule.
* **(d) The benefits of Agile over the traditional (Predictive/Waterfall) approach for this project:**
  * *Action:*
    * *Early Value:* Customers get working banking features (e.g., account login) at the end of the first sprint, rather than waiting six months.
    * *Flexibility:* Financial regulations or competitor features can be integrated into the product backlog between sprints without expensive change control disputes.
    * *Reduced Risk:* Regular testing and reviews ensure bugs and usability issues are caught at the end of each sprint, preventing major system failures at launch.
